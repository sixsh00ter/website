1.5 Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites.

Copyright refer to the legal protection of original works authorship. This includes musical, artistic, written and other types of creative expression. This does not include industrial or technology invention although there are jurisdiction that consider computer software as creative work capable of copyright. Sometimes copyright protection overlaps with other types of intellectual property. The law in the UK is the copyright, design and patents act 1988. It similar to the US law in that it protects original creations involving literary work, music, art, film and sound. A supplemental law the copyright(Computer Programs)regulations 1992 extends protection to software which is normally protected under law and agencies unique from copyright. The UK law does not extend the same copyright protection to logos as the US law. You will have to register any logos as a trademark
